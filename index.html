Hybrid Computational Models of Spinal Cord Stimulation

Technologies: MATLAB, Python, COMSOL Multiphysics, NEURON

Built from scratch a hybrid multi-scale computational model of the thoracic spinal cord to study neural recruitment under transcutaneous and epidural electrical stimulation, bridging finite element physics with biophysical neural dynamics.

  • FEM geometry & physics — Designed and meshed the full spinal cord geometry in COMSOL, assigning tissue-specific conductivities and boundary conditions to simulate the electric field distribution under both transcutaneous and epidural electrode configurations.
  • Neural modeling pipeline — Imported the computed extracellular potentials into NEURON and implemented biophysically realistic axon models to simulate action potential recruitment across dorsal column and dorsal root fibers.
  • Multi-scale coupling — Developed the full Python/MATLAB pipeline to automate the data transfer between COMSOL and NEURON, enabling systematic parameter sweeps across stimulation amplitudes, frequencies, and electrode positions.
  • Recruitment & selectivity analysis — Quantified fiber-type selectivity and recruitment curves across stimulation configurations, identifying conditions that maximize dorsal column activation while minimizing off-target recruitment.
  • Translational framing — Engaged with clinicians to ground model parameters in realistic therapeutic scenarios and ensure findings were interpretable in a clinical context.

Kirigami-Based Soft Implantable Electrodes

Technologies: Electrochemistry, Impedance Spectroscopy, Material Testing, Data Analysis

Independently investigated the long-term electrochemical and mechanical stability of kirigami-inspired soft electrodes intended for chronic neural implantation, running the full experimental pipeline from ageing protocols to data interpretation.

  • Ageing protocol design — Designed and executed in vitro accelerated ageing studies, exposing electrodes to physiologically relevant conditions over extended timescales to simulate chronic implantation.
  • Impedance spectroscopy — Performed electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) measurements at multiple time points to track impedance evolution and identify degradation onset and progression.
  • Degradation analysis — Characterized material failure mechanisms — including delamination, coating breakdown, and geometric deformation of the kirigami cuts — and linked them to measurable changes in the electrochemical response.
  • Suitability assessment — Synthesized experimental findings into a quantitative assessment of electrode longevity and reliability, evaluating readiness for chronic in vivo deployment.

Mapping the Collective Reddit Brain

Technologies: Python, Network Analysis, Data Visualization

We treated Reddit as a nervous system — subreddits as neurons, hyperlinks as action potentials, and linguistic co-activation as functional connectivity — asking whether a platform of millions behaves like a brain.

  • Interaction networks — Built a structural connectome from hundreds of thousands of hyperlinks, classifying communities into sensory, interneuron, and motor archetypes by in/out-degree, with 300-dimensional embeddings as "axon length" proxies.
  • NLP & feature extraction — Compressed LIWC psychological signals into six coherent neural signals per subreddit (affect, cognition, social orientation, etc.) to capture meaningful dynamics across time.
  • Functional vs. structural connectivity — Isolated subreddits that co-evolve without ever directly linking, analogous to fMRI connectivity beyond anatomical wiring.
  • Visualizations — Interactive dashboards for sentiment flow, hub resilience, and a 3D brain projection mapping subreddit categories onto MNI brain regions.

My Contributions:

  • Led the project and coordinated the team across all research directions
  • Originated the core concept: representing Reddit's structure as a brain and mapping it onto a neurological atlas
  • Drove the integration of external datasets to enrich the network with real-world context
  • Designed the neuron archetype analysis: asking whether subreddit categories behave like sensory, interneuron, and motor regions based on their linking patterns
  • Conceptualized the Positive & Negative Circuits framework to identify amplifying and dampening dynamics in the network
  • NLP & feature extraction to create the clustering in categories

Graphene Bubble Lens — Electromechanical Simulation

Technologies: COMSOL Multiphysics, Electrostatics, Solid Mechanics, FEM

Modelled the voltage-induced deformation of graphene bubble lenses using multiphysics finite-element simulation, treating the bubble as a tunable nano-optical element whose geometry is controlled by an applied gate voltage.

  • Geometry & physics setup — Built circular (2D axisymmetric + 3D) and triangular (pyramidal 3D) lens geometries in COMSOL, coupling Electrostatics and Solid Mechanics interfaces with parametric voltage sweeps from 0 to 400 V.
  • Voltage vs. deflection — Simulated central deflection as a function of gate voltage, bubble diameter, and SiO₂ thickness; thinner dielectric layers produced up to 300 nm deflection vs. ~20 nm for thick ones, consistent with the capacitor-force model.
  • Stress distribution — Analysed von Mises stress maps showing maximum stress concentration at clamped edges, with symmetric patterns for circular and corner-localized peaks for triangular geometries.
  • Modal / eigenfrequency analysis — Extracted resonant modes for both lens shapes; results confirm out-of-plane fundamental modes with higher-order nodal patterns at increased frequencies.

My Contributions:

  • Led the project and coordinated the team across all research directions
  • Built and debugged the full 2d-axisymmetric and 3D COMSOL geometry for the circular and triangular lens, resolving memory assembly issues by finding the minimum viable graphene thickness (6 nm)
  • Ran parametric sweeps across voltage, bubble diameter, and SiO₂ thickness and interpreted the dielectric-spacing effect on electrostatic force
